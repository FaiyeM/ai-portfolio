# APRA CPS 234 — Information Security (Summary)

**Framework:** APRA Prudential Standard CPS 234  
**Regulator:** Australian Prudential Regulation Authority (APRA)  
**Effective:** July 2019  
**Applies to:** ADIs, general insurers, life companies, RSE licensees, and private health insurers

---

## Overview

CPS 234 requires APRA-regulated entities to maintain information security capability commensurate with the size and extent of threats to their information assets. The standard establishes minimum requirements across governance, control frameworks, third-party risk, and incident response.

---

## Information Security Capability (Paragraph 15–20)

Entities must maintain information security capability that is **commensurate with the size and extent of threats** to their information assets. This includes:

- Maintaining an **Information Security Policy** that is reviewed and approved by the Board at least annually
- Ensuring the Board and senior management maintain **active oversight** of information security
- Defining **roles and responsibilities** for information security at all levels of the organisation
- Ensuring staff have **appropriate skills and resources** to maintain information security

The Board is ultimately responsible for ensuring the entity maintains adequate information security capability.

---

## Information Asset Identification and Classification (Paragraph 21–24)

Entities must classify information assets — including those managed by third parties or related parties — based on **criticality and sensitivity**. Classification must:

- Be performed for **all information assets** across the organisation
- Support appropriate **access controls** commensurate with classification
- Be reviewed whenever there are **material changes** to the asset or business context

---

## Implementation of Controls (Paragraph 25–32)

Entities must implement **controls to protect information assets** commensurate with their criticality and sensitivity. Controls must be:

- **Designed** to protect against internal and external threats
- **Tested** for operating effectiveness on a schedule commensurate with risk
- Addressing vulnerabilities including those introduced by **third-party arrangements**

Penetration testing must be performed at least annually for internet-facing systems and whenever material changes occur.

---

## Incident Management and Notification Obligations (Paragraph 33–39)

Entities must have mechanisms to **detect and respond** to information security incidents promptly.

### Notification to APRA

Entities must notify APRA **as soon as possible and no later than 72 hours** after becoming aware of:

- An information security **incident that materially affected or could affect** the entity or interested parties
- An information security **control weakness** that the entity expects cannot be remediated in a timely manner

### Internal Escalation

The Board and senior management must be notified promptly of information security incidents and control weaknesses.

---

## Third-Party Risk Management (Paragraph 40–44)

Entities remain **responsible** for information security of assets managed by related parties or third parties. Requirements include:

- Maintaining visibility of information assets managed by third parties
- Ensuring third parties have **adequate information security controls**
- Including **contractual requirements** for third parties to notify the entity of security incidents
- Regularly assessing third-party information security controls through audit or attestation

---

| Event Type | Notification Timeframe | Recipient |
|------------|----------------------|-----------|
| Material information security incident | Within 72 hours | APRA |
| Control weakness expected to remain unremediated | As soon as possible | APRA |
| Incident affecting interested parties | Within 72 hours | APRA + affected parties |

## Common Compliance Questions

**Q: What constitutes a "material" information security incident under CPS 234?**  
A: APRA considers an incident material if it has or could adversely affect the financial standing, reputation, or operations of the entity, or if it affects customers or other interested parties.

**Q: Does CPS 234 require penetration testing?**  
A: Yes. Penetration testing is required at least annually for internet-facing systems. Tests must be conducted by competent, independent assessors.

**Q: How does CPS 234 address cloud services?**  
A: Cloud arrangements are captured under the third-party management requirements. Entities must maintain oversight of information security regardless of whether assets are managed on-premises or by a cloud provider.
